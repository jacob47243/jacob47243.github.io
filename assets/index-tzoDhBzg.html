Chess-Playing Robotic Arm

A ROS2-driven arm that recognizes the board, plans legal moves with Stockfish, and executes precise pick-and-place motions with an IK solver and failure recovery.

Hardware

  • 5 DOF arm + gripper; links ≈ 0.45 m and 0.40 m (covers full 75×75 cm board).
  • Motors: base X8-9, shoulder X8-16, elbow X5-9, wrist X5-4, gripper X5-4.
  • Aluminum construction for stiffness and accuracy.

IK & Control

  • Newton–Raphson IK with weighted inverse Jacobian for stability near singularities.
  • “Elbow-up” constraints + wrist-down orientation to avoid table collisions.
  • Error-map XY offsets and gravity compensation (shoulder & elbow) for precision.

Perception

  • Two cameras: player-view (FEN via Chessify) and overhead (board & piece localization).
  • Aruco-based world mapping; fallen-piece detection and indicator tracking.

Software (ROS2)

  • FenDetectorNode → capture, Chessify upload, FEN string.
  • BoardDetectorNode → continuous top-down detection & world coords.
  • PlayGameNode → loop + Stockfish move generation.
  • TrajectoryNode → segment execution & wait-state signaling.
