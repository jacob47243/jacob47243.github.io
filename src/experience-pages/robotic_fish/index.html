Robotic Fish — Bio-Inspired Underwater Propulsion

Built a biomimetic fin propulsor that achieves large-angle fin tilts via a swash-plate mechanism and universal joint, driven by a high-torque AK60-6 motor. Control is split across Arduinos (servos and central motor) with CAN/UART comms; trajectories are generated in MATLAB and sent as joint targets. Goal: efficient, collinear-flow propulsion with future ML-optimized fin trajectories.

Mechanism & Build

  • Swash-plate + ball-and-socket universal joint to transmit rotation while allowing ~30° fin tilt.
  • Motor moved behind the swash plate; shafts route through the baseplate to the joint.
  • 3D-printed swash-plate arms and adapter parts for manufacturability and sealing.

Actuation & Control

  • AK60-6 for central drive; servos for swash-plate actuation.
  • CAN bus comms to the motor; MATLAB → Arduino pipeline for trajectories and IK.
  • Two-MCU architecture to avoid comms interference (servos vs. central motor).

Testing & Next Steps

  • Validated kinematics and control on the bench; prepared watertight nose with o-ring seal.
  • Plan: underwater testing, integrate external force sensor, and use ML to optimize fin trajectories.
